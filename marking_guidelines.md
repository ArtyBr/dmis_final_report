# Marking Guidelines for Final Report

## Technical Content

The purpose of this section is to focus on the marking scheme for the "technical content" section of the assessment criteria.

It is probably helpful initially to note that there are typically two types of project. The first, a "practical project", typically involves the student developing a piece of software or hardware, and following a process (such as a "waterfall" software lifecycle) in order to facilitate the development. The second, a "research project", involves an in-depth investigation of a topic with a clear outcome at the end of it, and will be guided by a research methodology. We will consider each type of project separately.

The following two descriptions of what should be contained within a project report are adapted from the "Guidelines on Course Exemption and Accreditation", section 4.5, BCS, Swindon, 1998.

### Practical Projects

The project report should normally include:

- elucidation of the problem and the objectives of the project;
- an in-depth investigation of the context/literature/other similar products;
- a clear description of the stages of the life cycle undertaken;
- a description of the use of appropriate tools to support the development process;
- a description of how verification and validation were applied at all stages;
- a critical appraisal of the project, indicating the rationale for design/implementation decisions, lessons learnt during the course of the project, and evaluation (with hindsight) of the product and the process of its production;
- references;
- appendices (including code and technical documentation).

### Research Projects

The project report should normally include:

- elucidation of the problem or research area and the objectives of the project;
- an in-depth investigation of the context/literature/other similar products;
- a clear description of, and rationale for, the research methods being used;
- a description of the outcome of the research;
- a critical appraisal of the project indicating the rationale for the methods used, lessons learnt during the course of the project, and evaluation (with hindsight) of the outcome;
- references.

### Other Projects

Most projects can be viewed either as practical or as research, but some will contain both practical and research material. A project which does overlap these two categories should not present a problem for marking purposes, since the division between research and practical activities should be apparent to the marker.

### Allocating Marks

| Percentage Mark | Mark Description |
|---|---|
| 85%-100% | Outstanding - close to perfect in most respects; the student has shown a deep understanding and appreciation of the project material, and the project objectives have been achieved to an exceptionally high standard |
| 70%-84% | Exceptionally good - consistently of a high standard with few weaknesses; the student has shown a thorough understanding and appreciation of the project material, and the project objectives have been achieved |
| 60%-69% | Very good - consistently of a high standard with no serious weaknesses; the student shows a clear understanding of the technical and professional issues, and has made good progress toward the project's objectives |
| 50%-59% | Good - sound overall, possibly some weak aspects but offset by some very good aspects; the project is competent in most respects, and the project objectives should have been at least partially achieved |
| 40%-49% | Satisfactory - sound overall, possibly some very weak aspects and some very good aspects; the student has demonstrated a basic understanding of the project material and has made fair progress towards the project objectives |
| 30%-39% | Weak - barely adequate overall, serious weaknesses in some aspects; some work has been done, but insufficient progress towards the project objectives has been made |
| 15%-29% | Very weak - inadequate, serious weaknesses in some aspects; there is some correct and relevant material, but many issues are either neglected or are covered incorrectly |
| 1%-14% | Very poor - seriously inadequate in some essential respects; very little correct or relevant material and no real appreciation of the project requirements |
| 0% | No submission |

The normal expectation is for a mark of at least 60% if the student is working effectively. A mark of less than 40% indicates that something has gone seriously wrong.

It is important that you identify the strengths and weaknesses of the project (and mention them in the feedback) so that the mark awarded is evidenced.

You should also bear in mind that some projects may be more difficult than others, and your marks for this section should reflect the technical challenges associated with the project.

## Communication Skills

The purpose of this section is to focus on the marking scheme for the "communication skills" section of the assessment criteria.

### Allocating Marks

| Percentage Mark | Mark Description |
|---|---|
| 85%-100% | Outstanding - close to perfect in all respects; the report is a model technical document |
| 70%-84% | Exceptionally good - consistently of a high standard with few weaknesses; the report displays excellent organisational and presentational skills, but improvements can be identified |
| 60%-69% | Very good - consistently of a high standard with no serious weaknesses; the report is written well and structured appropriately, but significant areas for improvement can be identified |
| 50%-59% | Good - sound overall, possibly some weak aspects but offset by some very good aspects; the presentation of the report is reasonably clear |
| 40%-49% | Satisfactory - sound overall, possibly some very weak aspects and some very good aspects; the presentation of the report is acceptable, but may contain substantial structural or linguistic imperfections |
| 30%-39% | Weak - barely adequate overall, serious weaknesses in some aspects; the report is readable, but only just, and may omit required components |
| 15%-29% | Very weak - inadequate, serious weaknesses in some aspects; the report fails to communicate to the reader what the student has achieved |
| 1%-14% | Very poor - inadequate, serious weaknesses in many aspects; the report fails to communicate to the reader what the student has achieved, and either omits required components or contains serious linguistic errors |
| 0% | No submission |

The normal expectation is for a mark of at least 60% if the student is working effectively. A mark of less than 40% indicates that something has gone seriously wrong.

It is important that you identify the strengths and weaknesses of the project (and mention them in the feedback) so that the mark awarded is evidenced.
